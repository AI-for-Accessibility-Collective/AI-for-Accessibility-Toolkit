    COGNITIVE ACCESSIBILITY

    The Cognitive Load Conundrum: When Websites Overwhelm the Mind

    The proliferation of contemporary digital interfaces has engendered a paradigmatic shift in the phenomenological experience of information consumption, whereby users with neurodivergent conditions—including but not limited to attention deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), autism spectrum disorder (ASD), and various manifestations of cognitive impairment—encounter increasingly insurmountable barriers to effective web navigation. The implementation of extraneous visual stimuli, non-hierarchical information architecture, and the conspicuous absence of progressive disclosure mechanisms collectively contribute to an environment of cognitive overload that fundamentally undermines the accessibility aspirations enumerated in the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines.
